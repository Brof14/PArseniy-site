I grew up in Malougrenyovo, a village in Altai, Russia. There wasn't much around except school, fields, and the internet, so that's where I spent most of my time.

I got my first computer at 12 and started learning Python at a local programming academy. At 14, I stopped treating programming as writing code line by line and started treating it as building products. AI became part of my workflow, allowing me to move from an idea to a working product much faster.

That led to ByMeVPN, my first startup. I built the product, launched it, found my first customers, and learned what it actually means to maintain software when things break, providers change, and network restrictions appear.

I chose to spend 40 minutes on a bus every morning to attend a stronger school in Biysk instead of the one ten minutes from home. Most of those trips are spent reading, thinking, or working on ideas for whatever comes next.

Right now, I'm building my next startup while preparing to study Computer Engineering at Politecnico di Milano. My long-term goal is to build AI startups that compete globally.
